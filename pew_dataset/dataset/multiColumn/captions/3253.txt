The perceived requirements of being a true Australian notwithstanding, the prevailing view (49%) among the Australian public is that growing cultural diversity makes their country a better place to live. By comparison, a majority of Americans (56%) agree that having an increasing number of people from many different races, ethnic groups and nationalities is a positive for the U.S. In Europe, however, a median of just 22% across 10 European countries surveyed say that growing diversity makes their countries a better place to live.
