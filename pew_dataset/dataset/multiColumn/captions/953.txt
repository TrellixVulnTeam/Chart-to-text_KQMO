Despite their differing views of abortion and other religious issues, Americans generally see the high court as impartial toward religion. Seven-in-ten U.S. adults (69%) say the court is neutral toward religion, while 18% say the court is friendly toward religion and 11% say it is unfriendly, according to a 2019 survey. Majorities of both Republicans (75%) and Democrats (65%) – as well as Christians (72%) and religious “nones” (65%) – say the court is neutral toward religion. The survey also found that 76% of white Protestants who are not evangelical, 72% of Catholics and 72% of white evangelical Protestants see the court as neutral toward religion.
