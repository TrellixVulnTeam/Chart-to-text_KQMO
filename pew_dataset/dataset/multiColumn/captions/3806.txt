Generally, those who are most concerned about Zika include older adults, especially older women, and those who report “fair” or “poor” health status. For instance, 44% of women ages 50 and older think Zika is a major threat to the U.S. population as a whole, compared with 33% of older men. By contrast, 23% of adults under age 50 consider Zika a major threat to the population. Additionally, 42% of those who report being in “fair” or “poor” health feel it is a major threat vs. 27% of those who say they are in “excellent” or “very good” health.
