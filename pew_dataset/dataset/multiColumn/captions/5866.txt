For those ages 50 and older, television remains the dominant source for news. About three-quarters (77%) of those 50-64 say television is a main source for news, compared with 38% who cite the internet. For those ages 65 and older, 84% turn to television for most of their news, 54% rely on newspapers and just 18% say the internet is a top news source. For both those ages 50-64 and 65 and older, there has been no significant increase in the percentages citing the internet as a main source for news since 2011.
