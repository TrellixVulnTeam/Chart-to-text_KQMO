While wary about U.S. global involvement, there has been a shift in Americans’ views since Russia’s challenge to the pro-Western government in Ukraine, and the rapid rise of ISIS in the conflict-ridden Middle East. Today, 39% of Americans say the U.S. is doing too much to solve world problems, a drop from the 51% who said the same last November. Those saying that the U.S. does too little to address global problems has nearly doubled, from 17% to 31%.
