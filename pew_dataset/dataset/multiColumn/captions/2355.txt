The public sees China in less negative terms than Russia. Still, 43% consider China a serious problem (but not an adversary); about a third (34%) say China is not much of a problem, while just 16% consider China an adversary. The share that considers China an adversary has edged down 6 points since early 2017.
Adults younger than 50 are less likely than older adults to see China as a problem. Nearly seven-in-ten (68%) of those ages 50 and older view China as a serious problem (49%) or as an adversary (19%). A smaller share of those ages 18 to 49 (51%) think of China as a serious problem (39%) or as an adversary (13%); 44% of younger adults consider China not much of a problem.
Among Republicans and Republican leaners, 68% say China is a serious problem (46%) or an adversary (22%). A smaller majority of Democrats and Democratic leaners see China in these terms (54%), including just 11% who think of China as an adversary.
