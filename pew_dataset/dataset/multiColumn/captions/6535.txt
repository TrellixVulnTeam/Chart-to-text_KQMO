As overall perceptions of economic news have grown more negative, the partisan gap in perceptions has widened. In the new survey, 64% of Republicans say news about the economy is mostly bad, compared with 44% of independents and 40% of Democrats.
In January, when views of economic news were much less gloomy, the partisan differences were much smaller: 24% of Republicans, 17% of Democrats and 28% of independents said news about the economy was mostly bad.
