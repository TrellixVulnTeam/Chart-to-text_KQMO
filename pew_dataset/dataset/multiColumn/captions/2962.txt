The public places a great deal of personal importance on the right to vote. When asked about the importance of five rights and freedoms, the right to vote ranks about as high as any item on the list. Overall, 91% of Americans say that the right to vote is essential to their sense of freedom. Only 7% say it is important but not essential, and just 1% says it is not important. A comparable share (92%) considers freedom of speech to be essential to their own sense of freedom.
Large majorities also say the right to privacy (87%) and freedom of religion (85%) are essential to them. Views are more mixed when it comes to the right to own guns: 48% say this is essential, while 33% say it is important but not essential and 19% say it is not important.
