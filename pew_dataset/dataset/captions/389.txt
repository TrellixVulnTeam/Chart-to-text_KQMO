Estimated spending by new foreign students at U.S. higher education institutions increased by about 184% from 2008 to 2016. In 2016, foreign students spent an estimated $15.5 billion on higher education – a total that includes tuition, room, board and other expenses – compared with $5.5 billion in 2008. Estimated total spending by foreign students at public universities grew by nearly 214% from 2008 ($2.5 billion) to 2016 ($7.8 billion).
Students from China, India and South Korea accounted for more than half (54%) of all new foreign students pursuing higher education degrees in the U.S. in 2016. About 108,000 new students were from China, accounting for 30% of the total. About 66,000 came from India (18% of the total), followed by South Korea with about 21,000 foreign students (6% of the total).
